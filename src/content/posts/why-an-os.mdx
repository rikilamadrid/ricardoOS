Most developer portfolios read the same way: a hero line, a grid of logos, a
timeline, a contact form. They're correct. They're also forgettable, closed and
gone in about fifteen seconds.

I wanted to build the other kind. So instead of a page, I made a place: a tiny,
glassy operating system you boot into and poke around.

## The bet

The bet is simple. **A list tells you what I did. A place shows you how I think.**

When you drag a window, swap the wallpaper, or stumble onto the terminal, you
learn more about how I build than any bullet point was going to tell you. The
medium is the message, and the message is "this person sweats the details."

## Restraint is the hard part

Frutiger Aero is really easy to do badly. It's one bad decision away from gaudy.
The whole challenge was keeping it calm: glass and gloss, sure, but in service of
something quiet and premium instead of loud.

If it makes one other person want to go build a better personal site, then it
did its job.
